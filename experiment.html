Equipment List

  • mg strip
  • Sulfuric Acid (0.5M to 1.5M)
  • Boiling Tubes
  • Vernier Temperature Probe
  • Sandpaper and Goggles
  • Beaker

Risk Assessment

  1. Sulfuric Acid: Irritant and Corrosive. Wear gloves and goggles at all times.
  2. Magnesium: Can irritate skin. Do not touch with bare hands.
  3. Glassware: Beakers are breakable. Handle with care to avoid cuts.

Method

Measure a fixed volume of 50mL of sulfuric acid by using a measuring cylinder and pour it into a scientific thermos.

Put in a temperature probe and start measuring in the Vernier app.

Add a fixed length of magnesium ribbon (15cm) when the temperature settles.

Record the data for 40 seconds and then stop the recording.

Calculate temperature change (final temperature − initial temperature).

Use temperature change to calculate the increase rate (temp. change/time).

Repeat the experiment for different concentrations (0.5 M → 1.5 M).

Repeat each trial and calculate the average for accuracy.
