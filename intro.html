Research Question

How does the concentration (0.5 M, 0.75 M, 1.0 M, 1.25 M, 1.5 M) of sulfuric acid affect the temperature change during its reaction with magnesium?

Hypothesis

As the concentration of sulfuric acid increases, the temperature change will increase.This is because higher concentration means more acid particles, so there will be more frequent collisions with magnesium, causing the reaction to release heat faster.

Background Research

Hydrogen is the simplest and most common element in the universe, usually found as a gas made of two atoms. It has an atomic number of 1 and comes in three types called isotopes: protium, deuterium, and tritium. On Earth, hydrogen is mostly found in compounds like water and can be separated using methods like electrolysis or other chemical reactions. It is very important in chemistry, especially for making ammonia, and it is also being studied as a clean energy source that can power fuel cells and engines, with the possibility of being used in fusion energy in the future. However, hydrogen can be hard to store and use because it has a very low boiling point and can react dangerously with oxygen. Today, scientists are working on ways to produce hydrogen using renewable energy to make it a more sustainable energy option.
